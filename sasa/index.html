NUTRITION

Nutrition is the science that studies the foods we eat and how they affect our health and well-being. A healthy diet provides the body with the essential nutrients it needs to function properly, including carbohydrates, proteins,fats, vitamins, and minerals. Carbohydrates give us energy to perform daily activities, proteins help in building and repairing muscles and tissues, and fats support brain function and provide long-term energy. Vitamins and minerals are vital for a strong immune system, healthy bones, and overall growth. Eating a balanced diet not only fuels our body but also prevents diseases such as diabetes, heart problems, and obesity. Proper nutrition is especially important for children and adolescents, as it supports growth and cognitive development. Additionally, drinking enough water and avoiding excessive sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats are crucial for maintaining a healthy lifestyle. By understanding nutrition and making smart food choices, we can improve our energy levels, mental focus, and overall quality of life leading to a happier and healthier future.

Protein

Protein helps in building and repairing muscles and body tissues. It is essential for growth, strength, and a healthy immune system. Foods like eggs, meat, fish, beans, and lentils are good sources of protein.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the main source of energy for our body. They help us perform daily activities and keep us active. Common carbohydrate foods include rice, bread, potatoes, fruits, and grains.

Fats

Fats provide long-term energy and support brain function. They also help the body absorb vitamins. Healthy fats are found in nuts, seeds, milk, butter, meat, sweet, deserts like choclate, and cooking oils.
