PROTEIN

Protein is a vital nutrient that plays an important role in keeping the body strong and healthy. It is required for building and maintaining body tissues such as muscles, skin, and organs. Protein also supports many internal processes that allow the body to function properly every day.

Unlike carbohydrates and fats, protein is not stored in large amounts in the body. This means it must be consumed regularly through daily meals to meet the body's needs. A consistent intake of protein helps maintain strength, balance, and overall physical well-being.

Benefits of Proteins

Proteins perform several essential functions beyond muscle development. They help repair damaged tissues, support cell growth, and assist in producing enzymes and hormones that regulate body activities. Adequate protein intake helps the body recover faster from physical stress. Other benefits include the following:

  • Helps repair and maintain body tissues
  • Supports the immune system and disease resistance
  • Plays a role in enzyme and hormone production
  • Maintains muscle strength and physical endurance

Protein Sources

Proteins can be found in a variety of foods, including animals and plants. Protein should be obtained from multiple sources. Some common sources include:

  • Eggs
  • Milk & Dairy Products
  • Fish
  • Beans & Pulses
  • Nuts & Seeds
  • Chicken & Meat

On average, these foods provide between 6 and 25 grams of protein.

Protein Deficiency

A lack of protein in the diet can lead to weakness, fatigue, and slow recovery from illness or injury. Long-term protein deficiency may affect muscle strength and growth, especially in children and physically active individuals.

Recommended Intake:

The recommended intake of protein depends on age, activity level, and health goals. For most adults, it is suggested to consume around 0.8-1.2 grams per kilogram of body weight daily. For individuals engaged in regular exercise, strength training, or trying to gain muscle, intake should be higher, around 1.2-2.2 grams per kilogram
