Fats

Fats are one of the three main nutrients required by the human body, along with carbohydrates and proteins. They are present in many everyday foods and form an important part of a balanced diet. Although fats are often misunderstood or avoided, they are necessary for normal body structure and basic biological functions. They are commonly divided into saturated and unsaturated fats based on their chemical structure.

Unlike carbohydrates, fats provide a slow and steady source of energy that helps the body function over longer periods. They play a key role in maintaining body temperature and protecting vital organs from physical damage.

Healthy Fats

Beneficial fats are mostly unsaturated fats that are good for the body. They help keep the heart healthy and maintain healthy skin. They help the body stay active and energized.

  • Olive oil & sunflower oil
  • Avocado & avocado oil
  • Almonds, walnuts, cashews
  • Fish like salmon & tuna
  • Seeds: chia, flaxseed, sesame

Less Beneficial (Unhealthy) Fats

These fats are mostly saturated fats which are not good for the body. They can increase cholesterol levels and raise the risk of heart disease. Excessive consumption of unhealthy fats can lead to weight gain and other health issues.

  • Fried chicken & fries
  • Burgers & fast food
  • Cakes, pastries & donuts
  • Butter, margarine
  • Packaged snacks & chips

On average, these fat sources provide roughly 5-20 grams of fat

Fats Deficiency

A lack of fats in the diet can cause dry skin, weak hair, vision problems, and low energy. It may also affect the absorption of important vitamins and impact brain and hormone function over time.

Recommended Intake:

The recommended intake of fats depends on age, activity level, and health goals. For most adults, it is suggested to consume around 0.5-1.2 grams of fat per kilogram of body weight daily. For individuals who are very active or trying to build muscle, intake can be slightly higher, around 1.5-2.0 grams per kilogram of body weight.
